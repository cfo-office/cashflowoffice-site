# Cash Flow Office Design Direction

## Brand Positioning
Cash Flow Office is not a bookkeeping site. It is a premium weekly financial operating system for residential builders, contractors, and project-based businesses.

The site should communicate:
- make more money
- protect margins
- reduce cash stress
- know what to pay
- know when to pay it
- run the financial week with clarity
- turn cash flow into an operating rhythm

## Visual Direction
Use the Spectra AR Hardware landing page as the visual inspiration:
- light blue / white spatial hero
- subtle architectural grid
- black elevated cards
- green accent glow
- floating cards that feel like they come off the page
- premium operating-system aesthetic
- mix light sections and dark sections as the user scrolls

## Keep From Current Cash Flow Office
- Darlene’s portrait
- Cash Flow Office name
- contractor positioning
- digital products section
- real cash scenarios section
- final CTA
- QuickBooks badge only as a small supporting credential near footer/about

## Hero Goal
The hero should feel executive, modern, and operational.

Suggested headline:
Run the Financial Week With Confidence

Alternative:
Cash Flow Office for Builders & Contractors

Suggested subheadline:
A weekly financial operating system that helps residential builders and contractors know what to pay, when to pay it, protect margins, and reduce cash stress before it becomes a crisis.

Primary CTA:
Book a Cash Flow Review

Secondary CTA:
Get the Free Cash Flow Sheet

## Product Cards
Make the two product cards feel premium and elevated.

Card 1:
Free Weekly Cash Flow Sheet
A starter worksheet to help contractors organize incoming cash, payroll pressure, upcoming bills, and weekly payment priorities.

Card 2:
Contractor Cash Control Spreadsheet
A premium spreadsheet system for weekly cash planning, pay cycle decisions, margin visibility, and short-term cash crunch prevention.
Price: $47

## New Section
Add a section called:
How the Financial Week Runs

Use workflow/activity cards:
1. Incoming Cash Review
2. Payroll & Vendor Pressure
3. Payment Priority Decisions

## Design Principle
This should feel like operational intelligence for contractors — not accounting, not bookkeeping, not a generic consultant website.
